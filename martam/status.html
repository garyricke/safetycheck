What we're building

SafetyCheck is turning Martam's Condensed General Training PowerPoint (20 slides) into a modern, mobile-first onboarding video that a new hire can watch on a phone before their first shift. Deliverables:

1 · Voice-over script

Human, safety-conscious, in Martam's family-owned voice — for the client to read & record. Chaptered for easy navigation.

2 · Branded imagery

Key-point visuals matched to the script, styled to Martam's real job sites and brand, sized 9:16.

3 · Assembled video

Captioned vertical cut synced to the recorded narration, delivered via GitHub → Netlify.

Client & voice assessment

Martam Construction Inc.

  • Founded 1973 by Tamas & Maria Kutrovatz; 50+ years, still family-run by their children.
  • Based in Elgin, Illinois. Heavy-civil / infrastructure.
  • Tagline: "Building in All Directions."
  • Core work: concrete paving (roads & runways), excavation & earthwork, structures & bridges, underground utilities.
#E5231F Martam red #232428 charcoal hi-vis accent

Voice we'll write in

Grounded, plain-spoken, and proud — a 50-year family company that looks out for its crew. Direct second-person ("you"), first-person-plural ownership ("here at Martam, we…"), zero corporate stiffness. Every rule tied to why it keeps you and the person next to you safe. Warm open + welcome, firm on non-negotiables (hard hats, fall protection, reporting).

Narration — voice source trial v1

Current build uses an AI-generated voice (not a human recording yet), chosen deliberately as a fast, flexible first pass so we can evaluate the fully assembled video before committing to a studio voiceover. The full script was narrated chapter-by-chapter and delivered as 11 MP3 files in /voice-overs (≈ 7:47 total).

Job-site environment (from real crew photos)

Thirteen real Martam job-site photos set the look for all generated imagery: underground utility & trenching work — excavated pits with shored trench boxes, excavators (Link-Belt, CAT), sewer/water installs, flagging and roadway zones, in leafy suburban Midwest settings. Crews wear yellow-green hi-vis vests with orange contrast, white/red/black hard hats, and the Martam-logo vest print. (One photo shows a SafetyCheck Confined Space Entry Permit and acknowledgment form — confirming SafetyCheck as the training provider.) Generated scenes will match this palette, PPE, equipment, and lighting for authenticity.
