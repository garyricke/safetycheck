The opportunity in one line

Turn safety trainings into a productized, trackable program SafetyCheck can sell to every client.

Martam proved the format: a short, phone-first safety video with clickable chapters and built-in knowledge checks, delivered on a branded page behind a login. The same machine can produce a library of these — fast and affordably — because Orbis's AI-assisted stack strips out the cost that normally makes custom training expensive. That low production cost is what leaves room for healthy margins at both companies while still pricing below traditional agencies.

Why partner instead of one-off projects: SafetyCheck owns the client relationships, the safety subject-matter expertise, and the trust to host the trainings. Orbis owns a repeatable production pipeline that turns a slide deck or a job site into a finished, tracked course. Neither is as valuable alone. Packaged together — video + tracking + hosting — it becomes a recurring product, not a series of invoices.

Two production formats

Same training page — two ways to fill it.

Every training uses the same interactive wrapper (video + chapters + quizzes + tracking). What changes is how the video itself is made. Offering two formats lets SafetyCheck meet different budgets and match the client's appetite for authenticity.

Format 1 · AI Studio

AI-produced video

The Martam pilot format · fastest & most affordable

  • AI voiceover (ElevenLabs) from an approved script
  • Photoreal AI imagery matched to the client's job sites & brand
  • Captioned vertical cut assembled in Descript
  • No filming, no scheduling around crews — quick turnaround
Best for policy/orientation content, fast rollouts, tight budgets Turnaround ~1–2 weeks
Format 2 · On-Site Filmed

Real iPhone interviews + b-roll

Authentic, client-specific · higher production effort

  • Real employees on real job sites, filmed mobile-journalism style
  • Their own supervisors' voices replace AI narration
  • On-site b-roll instead of generated images
  • Same chaptered, quizzed, tracked training page
Best for culture/buy-in, flagship courses, "this is us" authenticity Turnaround ~3–5 weeks (incl. a shoot day)

A useful myth-buster for client conversations: "filmed" is more expensive than AI here, but it is not the most expensive option — heavy 2D/motion-graphics animation often costs more than a real shoot ($7,000–$20,000+/finished minute). Mobile-journalism filming keeps the authentic feel while staying lean. The real savings story is AI voiceover + AI imagery vs. traditional production, not "animated vs. filmed."

The layer that makes it a product

Scoring & completion tracking — the compliance record is the real value.

For safety training, the video is only half the deliverable. What a company like Martam actually needs is proof: a record that each employee took the training, when, and how they scored. We've already built this exact system on Orbis's Hamstra roofing trainings — it ports directly onto the SafetyCheck pages.

What it does

  • Each learner logs in with their name before starting
  • Every quiz and module score is recorded, with timestamps
  • A completion certificate is generated with their name & final score
  • SafetyCheck gets a dashboard / export of who's done and who hasn't

Why it matters commercially

  • Audit-ready OSHA documentation SafetyCheck can hand its clients
  • Turns a one-time video into an annual, per-employee service
  • The same login that meters completion also meters per-seat billing
  • Creates stickiness — the client's records live in SafetyCheck's system

Already built, low cost to run. The Hamstra system uses a lightweight database (Supabase) that costs little to nothing at this scale. It's a modest one-time setup per client program, then a small recurring hosting line — but it unlocks the per-seat recurring revenue in the next section, which is where the real money is over time.

Reseller math, sanity-checked: a consultancy reselling a subcontractor's production typically marks up pass-through work 15–40%, or up to a 40–60% markup when white-labeling it as their own product. The AI Studio model above is a ~97% markup ($3,800 → $7,500) — aggressive on paper, but it holds because the customer isn't comparing to Orbis's invoice; they're comparing to a $9,000–$31,000 agency quote for a custom 9-minute safety course. Remember the trap: a 50% markup is only a 33% margin — the table prices to margin, not markup.

The part that compounds

Recurring revenue: per-seat tracked access + annual refresh.

The one-time video pays for the build. The recurring layer is what turns this into a business. Because the tracking system already logs each named learner, SafetyCheck can charge per employee, per year for access to the tracked training — and it's dramatically cheaper for the client than fragmented off-the-shelf courses, so it's easy to say yes to.

Per-seat access

$15–$30per employee / year — SafetyCheck → customer for tracked access

Anchored well below off-the-shelf OSHA courses ($50–$150 each) and fragmented per-topic vendors ($80–$120/employee). Easy value story.

Platform fee

$3–$6per seat / year — Orbis → SafetyCheck to run hosting + tracking

Or a flat ~$500–$1,000/yr per client program. Covers the database, hosting, and light maintenance. Leaves SafetyCheck the majority of the per-seat revenue.

Annual refresh

15–20%of production cost / year — content updates when rules or policies change

Billed on material change (a new chapter, a regulation update) rather than continuously. Industry norm is 15–25%.

Illustrative: a 150-employee client on tracked access at $22/seat/yr = $3,300/yr to SafetyCheck, of which a ~$4–5/seat platform fee (~$700/yr) goes to Orbis — recurring, on top of the one-time build. Across a dozen SafetyCheck clients, that per-seat layer quickly outweighs the production fees. These are planning numbers to react to, not a quote.

Packaging & incentives

Ways to package it for SafetyCheck's clients.

Single course

One video + tracking. The entry point — exactly the Martam pilot. Best for a first "yes."

Course library (volume)

A series of 4–8 videos for one client (e.g., Martam's crew-specific trainings). Offer ~15–20% off per video at 4+; production efficiencies grow with each one.

Program retainer

A monthly/annual retainer where SafetyCheck bundles hosting, tracking, and a set number of refreshes/new modules per year. Smooths revenue for both sides.

Market benchmarks behind the numbers

The baseline — what the market actually charges (2025–2026).

So the pricing above isn't guesswork. These are the anchors from industry rate guides and published rate cards. Expand each for detail and sources.

Training / explainer video — per finished minute & per project
  • Per finished minute: low/AI-assisted $500–$1,500; mid-tier custom $1,500–$5,000; premium/heavy animation $5,000–$15,000+.
  • Training video specifically: ~$1,000–$3,500/min typical → a 9-min course cross-checks to $9,000–$31,500 at agency per-minute rates.
  • Per project: low/AI $1,000–$5,000; mid-tier short video $3,000–$10,000; enterprise multi-module $25,000–$150,000+.
  • Custom safety video (project): ~$1,500–$6,000 for 2–3 min; VR safety $5,000–$12,000.

Sources: Advids, Vidico, Synthesia, Blue Carrot, DMAK Productions, Colossyan, Connective Web Design.

Voiceover — human vs. AI (the savings story)
  • Human eLearning VO: $30–$55/finished min, $0.20–$0.35/word, $350–$450 project minimum → ~$270–$700 for a 9-min script.
  • Human corporate narration (GVAA, 5–10 min tier): ~$600–$700.
  • AI VO (ElevenLabs): ~$2–$22 for the same 9 minutes (fraction of a $22/mo plan).
  • Net: AI VO is ~95–99% cheaper per video. (Confirm commercial-use rights at your plan tier.)

Sources: GVAA Rate Guide / Voice Crafters, Voices.com, The Voice Realm, ElevenLabs pricing.

Interactive eLearning — development effort & cost per hour
  • Chapman Alliance "hours to build one finished hour": Level 1 basic ~87 hrs; Level 2 interactive ~197 hrs; Level 3 advanced ~466 hrs.
  • Cost per finished hour: Chapman Level 1 ~$10,054; 2025 market avg ~$20,050 (range $6,740–$33,360).
  • Your video + chapters + quizzes sits at the Level 1.5–2 boundary — the interactivity is what justifies pricing above a flat explainer video.

Sources: Chapman Alliance, Kapp & Defelice, Leading Learning, Blue Carrot, AllenComm.

Filming — day rates & mobile-journalism vs. full crew
  • Solo videographer (published rate cards): half-day $350–$900; full day $650–$1,500 (e.g., DTX Media Dallas: $900 / $1,500).
  • Editing: $60–$150/hr; a 9-min multi-interview edit ≈ 15–35 hrs → ~$900–$5,000.
  • Mojo kit (phone, mics, gimbal) under $1,500 vs. broadcast ENG rigs $20,000–$50,000.
  • Filmed vs. AI: AI is far cheaper per minute, but for your model the realistic delta is ~3–8× on the production line (your AI version still carries labor).

Sources: Synthesia, Vidico, DTX Media, andrewproductions rate card, Ruah Creative House, c-istudios, Journalism University.

Hosting, tracking & per-seat safety pricing
  • SCORM Cloud (single course, by registrations): $40–$360/mo. Small-business LMS: iSpring $2.99/user/mo; TalentLMS $149/mo up to 40 users (certificates + completion stats included).
  • Onboarding/setup for SMB LMS packages: $3,000–$5,000 (we undercut this — the system is already built).
  • Off-the-shelf safety courses (per learner): OSHA-10 $50–$150; OSHA-30 $150–$300. Enterprise unlimited access under $35/employee/yr — the anchor our $15–$30/seat sits below.

Sources: Rustici/SCORM Cloud, TalentLMS, iSpring, OSHA.com, 360training, occupros.

Reseller margins & ongoing maintenance
  • Pass-through / subcontractor markup: 15–30% (labor +15–20%). White-label sweet spot: 40–60% markup; agencies target 60–70% margin.
  • Markup ≠ margin: a 50% markup is a 33% margin. Price to margin.
  • Annual maintenance: 15–25% of original development cost per year — the industry standard for keeping content current.

Sources: Parakeeto, The White Label Agency, Feedbird, DashClicks, Blue Carrot, TTMS.
